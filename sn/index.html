Factory Audit — Detailed Scope

📋 Simple Factory Audit

  • Factory profile (company registration, business license)
  • Detailed staff count and organization structure
  • Products currently produced
  • Observation of one production line (process steps, type and number of machines)
  • Overview of general organization (quality system, working environment, equipment condition)
  • Photos of factory building, production workshop, warehouse, office area
  • Photos of business license, export license, and any certificates

📋 Extensive Factory Audit

  • Factory profile & detailed staff count
  • Observation of one production line with capacity evaluation
  • A brief audit of the quality management system
  • A brief audit of social policy: child labour, forced labour, working hours (with random interviews)
  • A brief audit of safety policy: fire prevention, evacuation plan, chemical hazards, protective equipment
  • A quick assessment of environment policy: waste disposal, energy-efficient equipment
  • Photos of factory, workshop, warehouse, office, licenses and certificates

Are there any other fees behind the listed prices?
No any other fees behind the prices! All the prices you see on our website are all-inclusive prices. No hidden cost. It covers all related cost such as traveling, meals and report etc.
My factory recommended an inspection company. Should I use them?
Inspection companies should be 3rd parties. Factories only recommend inspection companies they have relationships with, which means the inspector is likely to pass a bad order. NovaSQC works exclusively for you, the buyer.
Why do the photos look different from what I sent?
When we take photos during a mid-production inspection or pre-shipment inspection, the goal is to ensure there are no discrepancies in size, material, coloring, shape, design, stitching, etc. Regarding shades of color, finishes, shine, and texture — these can be hard to capture via photos due to factory lighting, lack of sunlight, and camera angle. This is why we encourage clients to send in a product sample so our inspectors can physically compare it during the inspection.
What is a defect?
A product's nonfulfillment of an intended requirement or reasonable expectation for use, including safety considerations. There are four classes of defects:

• Class 1 (Very Serious): Leads directly to severe injury or catastrophic economic loss
• Class 2 (Serious): Leads directly to significant injury or significant economic loss
• Class 3 (Major): Related to major problems with respect to intended normal or reasonably foreseeable use
• Class 4 (Minor): Related to minor problems with respect to intended normal or reasonably foreseeable use
What certifications do your inspectors hold?
Our inspectors hold internationally recognized qualifications including API 1169, CWI, NACE, and ISO 9001. All inspectors have at least 4+ years of field experience. Certifications vary by region — contact us for details on specific inspector credentials in your area.
What languages do your inspectors speak?
Our inspectors speak English, French, Portuguese, Arabic, Swahili, Hausa, Amharic, Shona, Zulu, and more. We match inspectors to your project location to ensure clear communication with all stakeholders.
Can I cancel or reschedule an inspection?
Yes. You can cancel or reschedule your inspection up to 4pm local time the day before the scheduled date at no charge. For urgent reschedules, contact us directly and we will do our best to accommodate your needs.
